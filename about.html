About Me

I am Maximus A. Smith, a recent graduate of Kennesaw State University, where I earned my Master’s degree in Cybersecurity through the university’s esteemed Double Owl Program—an accelerated track that allowed me to complete both my Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Master’s in Cybersecurity concurrently..


During my time at Kennesaw State, I worked as a Graduate Student Research Assistant, contributing to cybersecurity-focused research initiatives that deepened my understanding of threat detection and secure system design. I applied principles of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and participated in best practices including unit testing, integration testing, and Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD) processes.


I am currently working as a Software Engineer at Genesis10, where I continue to sharpen my development skills and build on my foundation in cybersecurity. My long-term goal is to transition into the field of ethical hacking and security testing, using my combined knowledge of software engineering and cybersecurity to help organizations protect their systems and data.
